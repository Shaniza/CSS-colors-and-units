Difference between Hue, Saturation and Lightness
Hue Saturation Lightness
hue refers to the dominant wavelength of light that we perceive as color. It is the "pure" color without any tint (addition of white) or shade (addition of black) Saturation refers to the intensity or purity of a color. Lightness of a color can be described as how much light the color will have, where 0% means no light (black), 50% means 50% light (neither dark nor light) and 100% means full lightness (white).
Example:

Pure Red
hsl(0, 100%, 50%)

Example:

Black
hsl(0, 100%, 0%)

Example:

Grey
hsl(0, 0%, 50%)

Difference between rgb and rgba
rgb rgba
An RGB image refers to an image where each pixel is defined by the amount of red, green, and blue colors. In an rgba(red, green, blue, alpha) image, alpha parameter is a number between 0.0 (fully transparent) and 1.0 (not transparent at all)
